# Narration Script: Conjugate Gradient as Energy Minimization

## Scene 1: Title

Today we study conjugate gradient through its geometric meaning: minimizing a
quadratic energy. The algorithm is not just a clever recurrence. It is a
sequence of optimal corrections in the geometry defined by a symmetric positive
definite matrix.

## Scene 2: The Quadratic Energy

For a symmetric positive definite system $Ax=b$, define
$\phi(x)=\frac12 x^T A x-b^T x$. This function is strictly convex, so it has one
global minimizer. Its gradient is $Ax-b$, which vanishes exactly when $Ax=b$.

## Scene 3: Residual as Gradient

The residual $r_k=b-Ax_k$ is the negative gradient of the energy at the current
iterate. A large residual means the energy still has a steep direction of
descent.

## Scene 4: Steepest Descent Limitation

Steepest descent follows the residual direction. On elongated quadratic
contours, this often causes zigzagging. The method keeps revisiting directions
that were already partly corrected.

## Scene 5: Conjugate Directions

Conjugate gradient fixes this by using directions that are orthogonal in the
$A$-inner product. If $p_i^T A p_j=0$, then minimizing along $p_j$ does not undo
the minimization already achieved along $p_i$.

## Scene 6: Krylov Optimality

After $k$ steps, conjugate gradient chooses the best approximation inside
$x_0+\mathcal{K}_k(A,r_0)$. This is the affine space generated by the initial
residual and repeated multiplication by $A$.

## Scene 7: Convergence

The speed of convergence is controlled by spectral geometry. A smaller
condition number gives a better worst-case bound, and clustered eigenvalues can
make convergence even faster.

## Scene 8: Closing

The main message is that conjugate gradient is energy minimization, gradient
descent, orthogonalization, and polynomial approximation all at once. This is
why preconditioning is so powerful: it changes the geometry before CG begins.
